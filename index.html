    Mechanical Design and Compliance of a Residential
    Energy Management System            (2023-2024)

    Energy management Systems (EMS) are becoming increasingly prevalent and necessary for homes to achieve Canada's net zero goals in 2050, as they optimize energy usage, integrate multiple renewable energy sources, and enhance efficiency, thereby significantly reducing overall carbon emissions and supporting sustainable living.
    Implementing energy management systems in homes requires regulatory electrical safety compliance in the mechanical design stage is crucial to prevent hazards like electrical shocks, fires, arcing, etc. , which demands Compliance with relevant CSA and UL standards in tandem with thermal management and electromagnetic emmissions (EMI/EMC) considerations.

    Reliability/Thermo-mechanical multilevel Analysis
    of PCBs and ICs (2022)

    Solder fatigue is by far the number-one cause of microelectronics failure. The repetative heating and cooling of IC (thermal cycling) and CTE (coefficient of thermal expansion) mismatch between the IC and PCB makes the solder joint undergo cyclic shearing stress. Solder (predominantly Sn-Pb or Sn-Ag-Cu alloys) is a very complex material, undergoing viscoplastcity in high temperatures of IC operation. The successful fatigue model for such material is Darveax's plastic strain energy density model, taking into accont the plastic work of the worst solder joint in every cycle. This analysis requies rigorous non-linear (Anand's viscoplastcity) thermo-mechanical smulations to obtain the values for Darveax's model to perform a proper solder fatigue analysis. Besides, using explicit dynamics and modal analysis, other reliabilty aspects (harmonic and random vibration, PTH fatigue, thermal and mechanical shock) of PCBs and ICs were investigated. In addition, ANSYS Sherlock provides this capability to perform a board-level reliabilty analysis on all the components on the board, recognizing the reliability bottlenecks, and devising mitigation solutions aimed at purging the red (endangered) components list. This project was done for Daanaa Resolution's product. The original project is under NDA, the images above are taken from ANSYS Learning Hub.

    Optimization of Coanda Screen (CFD)            (2019-22)

    In colaboration with Sea-to-Sky Energy Solutions (S2SES), I proposed a novel design for the Coanda-effect screen in use in a hydroelectric facility near Whistler through extensive CFD Two-Phase Flow VOF simulations using ANSYS Fluent and SPH using LAMMPS, improving the efficiency up to ~70% while solving separation problems in flood times. This work eventually culminated in a patent.
